Where we stand

Otterbein is a compact historic rowhouse neighborhood between Hanover and Sharp, Barre and Henrietta — immediately southwest of the Inner Harbor. From the 500 block of South Hanover you are at the northern edge of Federal Hill, a short walk from both stadiums and the waterfront. Everything below is measured from Hanover & Lee — the center of the block — and is walkable in flat, urban miles.

I.

Bourdain Slept Here

519–521
S Hanover
Northeast Corner · Hanover & Lee

The townhouse on the corner — a one-minute walk from the front door.

Anthony Bourdain cooked at Gianni's in Harborplace in the early 1980s and lived, by the best available reckoning, in the three-story, four-bathroom townhouse on the northeast corner of Hanover and Lee. The deed traces back to seafood restaurateur Brice Phillips.

The Otterbein Community Association's research — Anthony Bourdain Slept Here — lays out the case in detail. It is, effectively, on the block.

II.

Suggested Loops

i

Morning Coffee & Climb

30–40 min · with dogs
  1. South on Hanover to Federal Hill Park; climb the hill, lap the summit.
  2. Down to Little Havana or a Key Hwy café for coffee on the patio.
  3. Inner Harbor promenade north, then back up Hanover.
ii

Evening on the Patio

45–60 min · with meal
  1. Stroll south to Blue Agave or Sobo Café for dinner.
  2. Loop home via Cross Street Market and S Charles.
  3. 0.5–1.0 mile round-trip; ample people-watching.
iii

Long Walk to Locust Point

60–90 min · workout pace
  1. Through Federal Hill, south on Key Hwy toward Locust Point.
  2. Off-leash time at Locust Point Dog Park, or daycare drop at Parkwood.
  3. Drink at a Locust Point bar; walk home along the water.

Compiled for the Johnson household — Otterbein, Baltimore.
